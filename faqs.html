Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about our hair restoration services, procedures, and aftercare.

How do I know if I am a good candidate for a hair transplant?+
Most healthy adults experiencing hair loss are candidates. Suitability depends on your hair loss pattern, donor hair availability, and overall health. Book a consultation for a personalized assessment.
What is the difference between FUE and DHI hair transplants?+
FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) involves extracting and implanting grafts individually. DHI (Direct Hair Implantation) uses a Choi implanter pen for more precise placement, often recommended for dense hairlines.
Is the procedure painful?+
The procedure is performed under local anesthesia, so you should not feel pain during the surgery. Mild discomfort or soreness may occur after, but this is usually manageable with medication.
How long is the recovery period?+
Most patients return to work within a few days. Full recovery and final results can take several months as the transplanted hair grows and matures.
Will the results look natural?+
Yes, our experienced surgeons use advanced techniques to ensure your new hairline and density look completely natural and blend seamlessly with your existing hair.
How do I care for my hair after the procedure?+
We provide detailed aftercare instructions and ongoing support. This includes how to wash your hair, what to avoid, and when to resume normal activities.
